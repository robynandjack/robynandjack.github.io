Our story

University

Robyn and Jack both studied at Simon Fraser University. Over the course of their degree, the two attended most classes together, but being over-worked and under-caffeinated, didn't meet until the end of their program. They graduated together in the winter of 2013.

Work

Each year, work seperates the pair, as they head out to look for valuable minerals in the northern wilderness of Canada. While the mining industry is not known for its strong environmental record, the couple try and offset their carbon footprint by not bathing for three months.

Over the course of their relationship the couple have adventured to exotic destinations such as Mexico, Spain and Yorkshire. It was in Yorkshire where Robyn and Jack had the chance to experience a fascinating, primitive culture.

Hornby Island

Hornby is one of British Columbia's northern gulf islands, situated between Vancouver Island and the mainland coast. While most of BC's gulf islands can be reached by one or two ferries, Hornby requires three, and is worth every bit of the journey. The extra trip is what gives the island its culture and tranquility, and makes it a unique part of the west coast. That, and its sandy beaches. Although the island is a major tourist attraction during the summer, our love story with Hornby is defined by the cooler, quiet months of the off-season. It's this connection that led us to pick an October wedding at Sea Breeze Lodge, and why we're so excited to share it with our favourite people.

The ringside market is the hub of the island, hosting a collection of shops, food vendors, cafes and the co-op grocery store. It sits about half-way between the ferry terminal and Sea Breeze. Outside of summer, shops and stalls keep irregular hours, but the co-op is open seven days a week, all year round. Shoppers will find all of the daily necessities here, including a wide selection of groceries and a compact liquor store, which carries local and non-local options. A smaller store at Ford's Cove marina covers most grocery items as well, and serves fresh, home-made pizza. It is open from 8:00 AM 'til 8:00 PM, seven days a week. For travelers running on fumes, the Gas Bar serves coffee and gasoline.

How do I get there?

Getting to the island may seem like a daunting trip, but consists of very little time on the road, and lots of time spent watching the coastal scenery from a ferry. Step one is getting to Vancouver Island, accomplished by catching the Departure Bay ferry from Horseshoe Bay, or the Duke Point ferry from Tsawwassen.

Departure Bay Ferry
Duration: 1 hour 40 minutes
Arrives: Departure Bay, Nanaimo
Duke Point Ferry
Duration: 2 hours
Arrives: Duke Point, 20 minutes south of Nanaimo


From Nanaimo, step two is driving one-hour north to Buckley Bay, to catch the Denman Island ferry. Ferries depart about once every hour, with sailings synchronized for Hornby Island until 6:00 PM, except on Fridays, which has two extra Hornby-connecting sailings that run as late as 10:05 PM. Step three is driving across Denman Island, which takes about 20 minutes, to the Hornby Island ferry terminal, and taking the Hornby Island ferry. The fourth and final step is a short drive across Hornby Island to Sea Breeze Lodge.

To summarize in shorthand, the journey looks like this: take the Departure Bay or Duke Point ferry to Vancouver Island (1 hour 40 minutes or 2 hours) → drive north to Buckley Bay (1 hour) → catch the Buckley Bay-Denman Island ferry (15 minutes) → drive across Denman Island (20 minutes) → catch the Denman Island-Hornby ferry (10 minutes) → drive to Sea Breeze Lodge!

From Vancouver, the trip takes about six hours. Sailing times for the Buckley Bay-Denman Island route can be found here, and sailing times for the Denman Island-Hornby route can be found here. For a map with driving directions, scroll down.
